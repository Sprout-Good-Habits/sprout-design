Social Buttons
Third-party sign-in buttons that follow platform brand guidelines, displaying the provider's logo alongside the action label.
  • ✓ Use social buttons on authentication screens to let users sign in with their existing accounts.
  • ✓ Always show both Google and Apple options together for platform parity.
  • ✗ Don’t use social buttons for general actions — use the standard Button component instead.
  • ✗ Don’t restyle provider logos or change their brand colors — follow each platform’s brand guidelines.

Content

Social button labels must follow each platform’s branding requirements:

  • Use “Sign in with Google” or “Sign in with Apple” — the exact wording required by each provider.
  • Never abbreviate or alter the provider name.
  • Provider logos must use official brand assets. Google uses the full-color “G” mark; Apple uses the monochrome Apple logo.
  • Disabled state desaturates the Google logo to grayscale.

Accessibility

  • Keyboard: Social buttons are focusable and activated with Enter or Space. Use native <button> elements.
  • ARIA: Use aria-disabled="true" for disabled discovery by screen readers. Add aria-busy="true" during loading.
  • Contrast: Dark text (--text-secondary) on white surface passes AA at all sizes. Disabled buttons are exempt.
  • Touch targets: 48px height meets the 44×44px minimum touch target.
