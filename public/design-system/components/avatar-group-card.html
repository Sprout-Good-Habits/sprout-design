Avatar Group Card
A card displaying 2–5 Rive character avatars to represent relationships between individuals — teacher + kid, coach + kid, family, classroom, and more.
  • ✓ Use to visually represent a group of people who share a relationship, like a family or a class.
  • ✗ Don’t use for anonymous user counts or when individual identity doesn’t matter — use Avatar Group instead.

Use cases

Avatar Group Card is designed to represent real relationships between people in the app.

  • Teacher + Kid (2) — Coaching or mentoring relationship in an education context.
  • Parent + Kid (2) — Family pairing for parent-managed accounts.
  • Small family (3) — Parent and two children, or two parents and one child.
  • Family (4) — A household with parents and children.
  • Classroom / group (5) — A teacher with a group of students, or an extended family.

Anatomy

The component is composed of a sky-blue card container and a row of overlapping Rive character avatars.

  • Card container: Full width × 120px height, --radius-4xl (24px) corners, overflow: clip. Background is always --bg-sky — it does not change per variant or context.
  • Avatar row: Horizontally centered, positioned --spacing-3xl (24px) above the top edge so characters overflow into the card. Characters are always grounded — the bottom edge of the card clips their lower body, so they never float in empty space.
  • Avatar slot: Each avatar is 117×167px with --spacing-6xl (48px) negative margin for overlap on mobile. On tablet, overlap reduces to --spacing-xl (16px) to give characters more breathing room. The last avatar has no negative margin.
  • Rive character: Uses character2.4.riv with the Village-character artboard. Each avatar can have unique skin tone, hair, and clothing via state machine inputs. Always grounded — the bottom edge of the container always clips the character. It is never floating in empty space.

Variants

The card supports 2–5 avatars. The avatar row automatically centers within the card regardless of count. More avatars compress naturally via the overlap.

Accessibility

  • The card container must have role="img" and an aria-label describing the group (e.g., aria-label="Mom, Dad, and Alex" or aria-label="Ms. Chen's class").
  • Individual Rive canvases should have pointer-events: none — they are decorative within the card context.
  • When the card is inside an interactive container (e.g., a list item or button), the card is decorative and the container provides the accessible label.
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between characters and the --bg-sky background. The light blue works well with all character skin tones.
