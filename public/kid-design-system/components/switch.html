Switch
Switches toggle a single setting on or off. They provide immediate feedback and are used when the effect of the change is instantaneous.
  • ✓ For on/off settings like notifications, screen time, or privacy controls.
  • ✓ When the effect is immediate and doesn't require a separate save action.
  • ✗ Don't use a switch when the user is selecting from multiple options — use radio buttons or a list item instead.
  • ✗ Don't use a switch when the change requires confirmation — use a checkbox with a submit button.

Anatomy

  • Track (required) — 44×24px pill. Off: --bg-secondary. On: --brand-500.
  • Thumb (required) — 20px white circle that slides between left (off) and right (on).
  • Label (required) — Regular text-md in --text-primary. Placed to the right of the track.
  • Description (optional) — Regular text-sm in --text-secondary. Below the label.

Content guidelines

  • Labels should describe what happens when the switch is on, not the current state
  • Keep labels short — 1–3 words
  • Don't include "on/off" or "enable/disable" in the label — the switch visual communicates that
  • Descriptions should explain the consequence of the setting, not repeat the label

Accessibility

  • Role: Use role="switch" with aria-checked="true|false". Do not use role="checkbox".
  • Labels: Associate a visible label via aria-labelledby or aria-label.
  • Keyboard: Toggle with Space or Enter. Must be focusable with Tab.
  • Contrast: The brand-500 on-state track and white thumb meet WCAG AA. The off-state track (--bg-secondary) should have a visible border in high-contrast mode.
  • Touch targets: The 44×24px track meets the minimum 44px width. Ensure the tap area extends to include the label row.
