Task Card

Based on the sprout-app codebase. Task cards live on the kid home screen and represent a single daily task or habit. The card has five visual states across its completion lifecycle, and two physical formats depending on the child's age tier.

Components TaskCard, TaskListCard Formats 2 States 5 Source sprout-app / apps/child Role article
Note on "quests". There is no separate Quest card or data model in sprout-app. "Quest" is UX terminology used only in the task-detail flow (see apps/child/components/task-detail/QuestToolbar.tsx). Every card on the kid home screen is a task card driven by TaskWithStatus.
Section 1

State machine

Source: apps/child/hooks/child/useChildTasks.ts and apps/child/lib/task-visual.ts. The TypeScript type is TaskCardVisualState = 'available' | 'inProgress' | 'submitted' | 'completed' | 'rejected'. Only available, inProgress, and rejected are tappable.

inProgress

submitted

Tier 2 & Tier 3 — Task List Card (TaskListCard)
